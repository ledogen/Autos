Mass & Inertia

SymbolUnitsValueDescription
mkg1360Total vehicle curb weight. Governs linear acceleration: a = F_net / m. More mass → slower response but more momentum.
Ixxkg·m²~800Roll inertia — resistance to rotation about the longitudinal axis. Controls how quickly the body rolls in corners. Computed as (1/12)·m·(width² + height²).
Iyykg·m²~3300Pitch inertia — resistance to nose-dive under braking and squat under acceleration. Computed as (1/12)·m·(length² + height²).
Izzkg·m²~3700Yaw inertia — resistance to rotation about the vertical axis. High Izz makes the car resist direction changes; primary inertia in oversteer/understeer dynamics. Computed as (1/12)·m·(length² + width²).
I_wkg·m²1.22Rotational inertia of one wheel+tire assembly: I = ½·m_wheel·r². At 18 kg, r=0.368 m: I = 0.5·18·0.368² ≈ 1.22. Governs spin-up and lockup transients.

Drivetrain

SymbolUnitsValueDescription
T_dN·m800Peak drive torque delivered to the rear wheels (RWD) at full throttle. Converted to longitudinal force via Pacejka slip model. Excess torque → wheelspin.
T_bN·m1700Total brake torque applied to all four wheels at full brake input. Sufficient torque locks the wheels, transitioning from rolling to sliding contact.
T_hbN·m4000Handbrake torque — rear axle only. Intentionally high to lock rear wheels, destroying lateral grip and allowing the rear to rotate for drift initiation.

Tire Contact

SymbolUnitsValueDescription
FnNdynamicNormal force at the contact patch — the force the ground exerts perpendicular to the surface. Computed each step from tire compression: Fn = ks·Δ + cs·(dΔ/dt). All tire friction forces scale with Fn.
ksN/m100 000Tire radial stiffness. Spring rate of the tire carcass in the vertical direction. At rest each corner compresses ~22 mm: Δ = (m·g/4) / ks.
csN·s/m1 500Tire radial damping. Prevents the car from bouncing indefinitely. Critical damping ≈ 2 683 N·s/m (hub mass 18 kg); 1 500 gives ζ≈0.56 — below critical so the wheel returns to ground quickly without the premature unloading that over-damped values cause.
μ—0.9Peak tire-road friction coefficient. Scales Pacejka peak: F_peak = μ·D·Fn. Dry pavement is ~0.9; wet ~0.6; gravel ~0.5.
FlongNdynamicLongitudinal tire force — along the wheel's rolling direction. Positive = drive (pushes car forward). Generated by the difference between wheel surface speed and vehicle speed.
FlatNdynamicLateral tire force — perpendicular to the wheel's rolling direction (into the page in side view). This is the cornering force that keeps the car on a curved path.
Cr—0.015Rolling resistance coefficient. Horizontal drag = Cr · Σ Fn, directed opposite to velocity. Causes coast-down deceleration of ~0.15 m/s². Vanishes when airborne.

Pacejka Tire Model

SymbolUnitsValueDescription
B—10.0Stiffness factor. Controls the initial slope of the force-vs-slip curve. Slope at slip=0: B·C·D·μ·Fn/v_ref. Higher B → tire builds force faster from small slip.
C—1.9Shape factor. Must be in (1.0, 2.0) — the formula degenerates at C=2. Near 2.0 → sharper peak, faster falloff. 1.9 gives a rounded peak with gradual limit approach.
D—1.0Peak factor. F_peak = μ·D·Fn. D=1.0 means the tire fully exploits available friction. D<1 → tire underperforms; D>1 is physically unrealistic.
E—0.97Curvature factor. Near 1.0 produces gradual post-peak falloff — realistic tire behavior. Determines how quickly grip drops after the slip peak.
v_refm/s1.0Slip velocity at which the Pacejka curve approaches its peak. Normalizes the slip input: x = |s|/v_ref. Lower v_ref → tire peaks at smaller slip, feels snappier.
kL—1.0Longitudinal slip anisotropy scale. Multiplies the longitudinal slip component before computing slip magnitude. Default 1.0 (isotropic). Real tires: kL > kT by ~30–50%.
kT—1.0Lateral slip anisotropy scale. Multiplies the lateral slip component. Lower than kL in real tires — lateral carcass is softer than longitudinal.

Slip & Relaxation

SymbolUnitsValueDescription
ωrad/sdynamicWheel angular velocity. Surface speed at contact patch = ω·r. Integrated via Newton-iterated implicit Euler each step: I_w·Δω = dt·(T_drive − Flong·r − T_brake).
sLm/sdynamicLongitudinal slip displacement (relaxation-filtered). Positive = wheel faster than ground (wheelspin). Negative = wheel slower (lockup). Updated via implicit Euler: s_new = (s_old + dt·v_slip) / (1 + dt·|v|/L_r).
sTm/sdynamicLateral slip displacement (relaxation-filtered). Nonzero when contact patch slides sideways relative to vehicle heading. Drives the cornering force Flat via combined-slip Pacejka.
L_rm0.3Tire relaxation length. Characteristic distance the vehicle must travel for tire force to build after a slip change. Models viscoelastic lag in the tire carcass. Prevents force spikes at low speed.
