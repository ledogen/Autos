<objective>
Restore the perf the carve-and-meet approach lost AND make terrain a guaranteed-below decal bed for the
ribbon. The terrain carve's ONLY job is now: "terrain surface ≤ ribbonSurfaceY − clearanceMargin under a
footprint WIDER than the ribbon" — so terrain can never poke through the ribbon. It does NOT match the
ribbon's exact Y or fold in crown/camber (the ribbon carries those — visually via Plan 10, for physics via
road.js which is left intact).

This plan:
1. **Simplifies terrain.js `_buildCarveTable`** to O(1) per vertex: REMOVE the per-vertex
   `sampleDesignGradeAt` (binary search), the 2nd `queryNearest` (camber), and the per-vertex closure
   allocation. Compute a cheap per-tile/per-segment design-grade target once, reuse it across the tile's
   vertices, and carve toward `targetY − clearanceMargin`. Drop crown/camber/pothole fold-in from the
   terrain mesh.
2. **Widens the carve footprint** by `roadCarveExtraWidth` beyond `roadHalfWidth + roadShoulderWidth`.
3. **Keeps the cut/fill toe + carveBlend** so the depression rejoins terrain continuously (SURF-05).
4. **Leaves road.js `_sampleCarveWorld` intact** — physics still samples the unified ribbon surface
   (smoothed grade + crown + camber + pothole) on-road, blending to terrain off-road. The truck drives the
   visible ribbon, not the trough floor.
5. Ensures the cheap carve target is continuous across tile boundaries (no new longitudinal seam step).

Purpose: kill the ~1 s in-sim terrain hang (perf regression from 09-08) and guarantee terrain ≤ ribbon
under a wide footprint so the decal ribbon never has terrain poking through it.
Output: a cheap below-margin carve, two new params + sliders, untouched physics ribbon sampling.
</objective>
