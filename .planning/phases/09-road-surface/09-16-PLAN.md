<objective>
Replace the per-vertex `queryNearest` + 4-corner `bilinearGrade` in `terrain.js _buildCarveTable`
with a pre-sampled spline-point lookup. ONE rework fixes both confirmed gaps:

1. LAG — `_buildCarveTable` calls `this._roadSystem.queryNearest()` for every terrain vertex
   (GRID_SAMPLES²=4225 per chunk). Each `queryNearest` runs `probeSpline`, sampling
   `spline.getPointAt(u)` 16–256× per call. Road-adjacent chunks = millions of curve evals,
   several streaming at once → the ~1s hang. (SURF-04)

2. ROAD-BELOW-GROUND — the carve cut target is a 4-corner-per-tile bilinear. On steep/curving
   tiles the road's mid-tile Y differs from corner interpolation, so the cut aims at the wrong
   height and terrain is not lowered enough → the ribbon gets buried. (SURF-05)

Both vanish when the carve target Y comes from the nearest PRE-SAMPLED spline point (accurate
per-vertex) found via a plain squared-distance loop (no curve evaluation in the hot path).

Purpose: Performance is the acceptance criterion — the inner loop must contain zero `getPointAt`,
zero `queryNearest`, zero per-vertex closure allocation, while the road stays above terrain.
Output: Reworked `_buildCarveTable`, a new `collectChunkSplinePoints` pre-sampler on RoadSystem,
and full removal of the `roadDebugLineOnSurface` viz toggle.
</objective>
