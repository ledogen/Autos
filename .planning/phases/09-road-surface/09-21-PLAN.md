<objective>
Compute ONE rate-limited camber profile per canonical run and have every consumer read from it (D2). `camberProfile(arcS)` samples signed curvature along the continuous run → applies a slew-rate limit (new tunable max °/m) → clamps to ±6°. Rate-limiting along the CONTINUOUS run (not per-slice, not per-vertex) means banking eases across slice seams and through curvature sign-changes — killing the clamp-flip spike at zero-crossings (bug #4). The ribbon sweep (road-mesh.js sweepRibbon), the terrain carve cross-section, AND physics (_sampleCarveWorld) all read camber from this one profile at their arcS. This replaces the per-vertex instantaneous `_splineCurvatureSigned` camber (road-mesh.js:206-208) and the second-queryNearest camber estimate (road.js:1485-1497).

Purpose: One camber source = visual and physics banking agree, and the rate limit removes the discontinuity. Cached and invalidated via D1's generation counter.
Output: camberProfile(arcS) on RoadSystem (cached/run-keyed/generation-invalidated); roadCamberRate param + slider; ribbon and physics rewired to read it.
</objective>
