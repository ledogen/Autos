<objective>
Make the terrain carve inherit the SAME cross-section as the ribbon (D3). The carve target per vertex becomes `roadY(arcS) + crownProfile(uLat) + camberTilt(uLat, camberProfile(arcS)) − clearanceMargin` — identical cross-section to the ribbon, lowered by the clearance. The trough then tilts WITH the ribbon → uniform clearance on banked turns (fixes bug #5: clip on the inside edge, gap on the outside edge; NUMERIC: 6°×5m = 0.52 m > 0.5 m clearanceMargin under a flat carve).

Plus the D3 refinement for switchbacks: bound each arm's carve footprint (shoulder + carveExtraWidth) to ≤ ½ the minimum inter-arm separation (set by the router min-turn-radius from D0) so adjacent arms' footprints don't overlap → no undermining/step by construction. Where geometry forces arms closer, resolve per-vertex by nearest arm with a max-floor guard: never carve a vertex below the floor the HIGHER arm needs there (a lower arm's cut can't remove an upper arm's support — accept a managed steep bank between arms).

Purpose: D0 already separates arms by > road width, so this is mostly a safety net — but the cross-section inheritance is the primary fix for banked-turn clip/gap.
Output: cross-section-inheriting carve target in _buildCarveTable reading camberProfile; footprint bound coupled to min-turn-radius; max-floor guard. Worker stays byte-identical.
</objective>
