<objective>
Kill the Phase 9 surface-layer regression that produces vertical STEPS at tile seams and a ~1 s terrain-load LAG. Drive the road ribbon (visual + physics) AND the terrain carve target from the CONTINUOUS routed centerline Y, and remove the per-tile `_smoothDesignGrade` smoothing that disagrees at slice boundaries and cache-misses on every stream. Make the cyan centerline viz draw the routed spline geometry (the truth), not the carve-inclusive `analyticHeight` surface.

Purpose: The Phase 8 routing spline is continuous and correct. The steps + lag are a Phase 9 regression: `_smoothDesignGrade` smooths the design grade PER TILE SLICE with arc-local end-truncated windows so adjacent slices disagree at their shared boundary, and slices are transient so the spline-keyed WeakMap misses every load and re-smooths (up to 512 noise evals/slice). The cyan viz draws `analyticHeight` (carve-inclusive) so a continuous spline LOOKS stepped.

Output: A road whose vertical profile is the continuous routed centerline, with `_smoothDesignGrade` no longer in the per-tile build/stream path (perf must improve), and a viz that tells the truth.
</objective>
