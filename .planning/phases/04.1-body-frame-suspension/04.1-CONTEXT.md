# Phase 4.1: Body-Frame Suspension - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-06-01
**Status:** Ready for planning

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Phase 4.1 corrects the coordinate-frame error introduced in Phase 4: hub state is currently a scalar world-Y position (`hubY[i]`, `hubVy[i]`), and the suspension spring acts purely along world Y. This is physically wrong as soon as the body tilts — the strut must compress along the body's local up axis, not world Y. The most visible artifact is that ARB equilibrium (compression-matched in world Y) does not correspond to the physical equilibrium (twist-zero in body frame), allowing the inside wheel to be lifted past the outer wheel's body-frame height during cornering.

Phase 4.1 also:
- Routes the full 3D tire contact normal force (currently only the world-Y component reaches the body, leaving the car parked on ramps under gravity).
- Adds bump and droop stops (intrinsically body-frame strut quantities).
- Fixes wheel-mesh visual binding (currently the axle-Z offset bleeds into body-local Y during pitch, displacing wheels visually).

Phase 4.1 does NOT change the Pacejka tire model, does NOT change ARB topology (still parallel-spring approximation per Phase 4 D-07), and does NOT introduce a 3D hub state — the hub remains a **rigid slider in body-Y** (constrained in body-X / body-Z; can only translate along the strut axis).

</domain>

<motivating_artifacts>
## Why This Phase Exists

Five artifacts observed during Phase 4 review (2026-06-01):

1. **Hub "floats" in flight (jump log `1780296563738.json`).** Already fixed in Phase 4 closure (implicit Euler → N=4 explicit Euler). Retain as regression check; not a Phase 4.1 item.

2. **ARB lifts inside hub past outer in body frame (rollover log `1780296509354.json`).** Root cause: `suspComp = L_S − (mountWorldY − hubY)` is the world-Y projection of strut length, not the strut length itself. ARB twist-zero is computed in this projection, so a rolled body produces an equilibrium that doesn't match the physical twist-zero. **Primary 4.1 target.**

3. **Suspension forces meaningless past ~45° roll.** Springs push along world Y, not along the strut axis. Once the body is on its side, the spring force is perpendicular to the strut and stops modeling anything. **Secondary 4.1 target.**

4. **Car parks on the ramp under gravity (log `1780375707695.json`).** Root cause: `suspension.js:298-304` only keeps the world-Y component of the tire contact normal force (`tireFz += tireFnAtContact * c.normal.y`); the horizontal (X/Z) component is discarded. `physics.js:270` explicitly forbids re-adding tire normal force to the body to avoid double-counting on flat ground. Net effect on the ramp: gravity Y is balanced by suspension Y, no horizontal force on the body, car sits still at 10° pitch indefinitely. **Must fix: transmit full 3D contact normal to body.**

5. **Wheel visual exaggerated by body pitch (same ramp log).** Root cause: `main.js:258` adds `(hubY - hubYRest)` (a world-Y delta) as the wheel mesh's body-local Y, then carGroup's quaternion rotates the result. The axle's local Z (±1.5 m) projects into world Y as `axleZ · sin(pitch)`, displacing front wheels up and rear wheels down by ~27 cm at 10° pitch. **Must fix: compute the wheel's world target position and inverse-rotate into body-local space.**

Plus: no bump stop and no droop stop. Currently the hub can travel arbitrarily far in either direction. Folded into this phase because stop positions are body-frame quantities.

</motivating_artifacts>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Coordinate frame & state

- **D-01:** Replace `hubY[i]`, `hubVy[i]` state with `strutComp[i]` (m, positive = compressed below rest length) and `strutCompVel[i]` (m/s, positive = compressing). 1D state per corner along the body-local strut axis. The hub is a **rigid slider in body-Y** (can only translate along the strut axis).
- **D-02:** Hub world position is derived, not state:
  `hubWorld = mountWorld + (L_S − strutComp[i]) · body_down_world`,
  where `body_down_world = (0,−1,0)` rotated by the body quaternion.
- **D-03:** Mount velocity along strut axis: project `(bodyVelocity + ω × rMount)` onto `body_down_world` to get `mountVelStrut`. Compression velocity `strutCompVel = mountVelStrut − hubVelAlongStrut`. Sign chosen so positive = compressing.

### Forces — strut axis (hub dynamics)

- **D-04:** Spring, damper, and ARB all act along the strut axis (body-local up/down). Magnitudes computed as in Phase 4 (`k·strutComp`, `c·strutCompVel`, `k_arb·Δstrut`); only the direction of application changes.
- **D-05:** Gravity on hub along strut axis: `m_u · g · (world_up · body_up)` projected component. When body is upright this is full `m_u·g` pulling the hub away from the body. When body is horizontal it tapers to zero. Lateral components of gravity on the hub are absorbed into the strut constraint (small-mass approximation; no inertial term modeled).

### Contact normal force routing — the ramp fix

- **D-06 (LOCKED, discussed 2026-06-01):** The tire contact normal force is a 3D vector. It splits cleanly along the strut axis:
  - **Strut-axis component** (= `tireFnAtContact · dot(c.normal, body_up_world)`) drives the hub ODE through the **tire spring** (replaces the current scalar `tireFz += Fn * c.normal.y`). The hub passes this Y force to the body via the existing suspension-spring pathway (`_suspForceAccum[i]` — unchanged accumulator route).
  - **X/Z residual component** (= `tireFnAtContact · (c.normal − dot(c.normal, body_up_world)·body_up_world)`) is transmitted **directly to the body** as a rigid-constraint force at the **contact patch world position**. Generates a **torque** on the body with arm `r = contactPoint − bodyCG`. The hub does NOT see this X/Z force (rigid slider constraint absorbs it instantly).
  - **Body never receives the raw contact normal Y directly** — only through the spring. Avoids the double-count that `physics.js:270` previously guarded against.

- **D-06a (LOCKED):** New accumulator `_hubNormalXZ[i]` (3D vector per corner) is populated inside `stepSuspensionSubsteps` alongside `_tireFz[i]` and `_suspForceAccum[i]`. Averaged across substeps (matches existing pattern). `physics.js` applies it after the substep call as `bodyForce += F_XZ` and `bodyTorque += (contactPoint − bodyCG) × F_XZ`.

- **D-06b (LOCKED):** Pacejka tire-load (`Fz` feed) remains the **strut-axis projection** (magnitude of the strut-axis component of the contact normal, post-substep average). This is the tire-spring force from the hub's perspective and remains consistent with Phase 4 D-03.

### Visual binding — the wheel-mesh fix

- **D-07 (LOCKED):** Wheel mesh world target = `(mountWorldX, hubWorldY, mountWorldZ)` where `hubWorldY` is the derived hub position from D-02. Then inverse-transformed by the body quaternion to obtain body-local position for the wheel mesh (since the wheel is a child of `carGroup`). Replaces the broken `wheelMesh.position.y = restLocalY + (hubY - hubYRest)` form in `main.js:258` which projected axle-Z through body pitch.

### Bump and droop stops

- **D-08 (LOCKED):** Suspension parameter shape on `data/ranger.js`:
  - **Main spring/damper** (existing): `suspensionStiffness{Front,Rear}`, `suspensionDamping{Front,Rear}`, `suspensionRestLength{Front,Rear}`.
  - **Total travel** (NEW): `suspensionTravelFront`, `suspensionTravelRear` — total distance the strut can compress before the bump stop engages. Separate front/rear sliders.
  - **Body offset** (NEW): `suspensionBodyOffsetFront`, `suspensionBodyOffsetRear` — Y offset of the mount point in body space (ride-height control). Currently mount Y is hardcoded as `-(cgHeight - wheelRadius)`; this offset shifts it. Separate front/rear sliders.
  - **Bump stop** (NEW): `bumpStopStiffness` (N/m) — penalty spring engaging when `strutComp ≥ suspensionTravel`. No pre-compression (zero force at the boundary, force grows linearly with overshoot). No separate bump-stop damping (main suspension damping handles it). Slider exposed.
  - **Droop stop** (NEW): fixed constant `DROOP_STOP_STIFFNESS` in `data/ranger.js`. Sized so it can cancel hub gravity for a hub mass ~2× the current `wheelMass` (≈36 kg → ≈350 N at engagement). Engages at the fully-extended boundary (`strutComp ≤ −droopRange`). No slider. Relies on main suspension damping for oscillation control.

- **D-09 (LOCKED):** Stop force model: linear penalty spring `F = k_stop · overshoot` past the boundary. Zero at the boundary itself (no precompression). Sums into the strut-axis force term alongside spring + damper + ARB. Force direction always pushes the hub back inside the travel window.

### Integrator

- **D-10:** Inherit Phase 4's N=4 explicit Euler substep choice. Re-verify the existing stability check still holds with `bumpStopStiffness` (≈10× main spring) folded into the effective stiffness when engaged. If `c_S · sdt / m_u` rises above 2 in the engaged regime, increase N.

### State initialization

- **D-11:** Static equilibrium at IC load / scenario reset:
  `strutComp[i] = (m · g · weight_i / 4) / k_S_i` per corner.
  Same hook used by Phase 4's `hubY` initializer. Replaces `vehicleState.hubY[]` / `hubVy[]` init with `strutComp[]` / `strutCompVel[]` init.

### Debug, logger, and visualization

- **D-12 (LOCKED):** Add to `wheelDebug[i]` and to logger output: `strutComp` per corner. Existing `fz` field unchanged. Logger schema bump per Phase 2 D-13 housekeeping convention.
- **D-13 (LOCKED):** New 4-corner suspension travel diagram in the debug panel: a simple bar visualization per corner showing the strut position relative to the droop stop (bottom) and bump stop (top). Bar fills proportionally to `(strutComp + droopRange) / (suspensionTravel + droopRange)`. Lives in the existing `initDebug` panel; minimal HTML/CSS or lil-gui display element.
- **D-14 (LOCKED) — new sliders:**
  - `suspensionTravelFront`, `suspensionTravelRear`
  - `suspensionBodyOffsetFront`, `suspensionBodyOffsetRear`
  - `bumpStopStiffness`
  Existing Phase 4 sliders unchanged.

### Migration / compatibility

- **D-15:** Existing logged fields (`fl_fz`, `fr_fz`, `rl_fz`, `rr_fz`) retain their meaning (tire spring force, strut-axis projection per D-06b). The 4 existing assertion scripts (`test/assert-m4-02/04/05/06`) probe these log fields only and require **no migration**.

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- **The hub is a rigid slider, not a 3D mass.** This is the key conceptual frame for Phase 4.1: the hub's only DOF is along the body-Y strut axis. All non-strut-axis forces on the wheel are transmitted instantly to the body through the rail constraint — they never appear in a hub ODE.
- **The body never sees raw contact normal Y directly.** It only sees the contact normal Y through the spring (via `_suspForceAccum`). The body DOES see X/Z directly (since the rail can carry those). This split is the conceptual key to avoiding the double-count guarded by `physics.js:270`.
- **Bump stop has no precompression.** Force is zero at the engagement boundary, linear past it. Mirrors how real bump stops behave.
- **Droop stop is "soft enough to be stable, stiff enough to feel like a stop."** Sized to a 2× hub mass so it doesn't visibly compress under the hub's own weight at full droop.
- **Travel bar in debug panel** — 4 simple per-corner bars, fill proportional to position within travel window. Bottom = droop stop, top = bump stop, middle band = current strut position. Lets you eyeball at a glance whether the suspension is bottoming out or topping out during driving.
- **Re-read assertion scripts before planning (D-18)** — confirm their physics still holds under the new architecture. Particularly m4-04 (load transfer) and m4-06 (damping) — both rely on `_fz` being a clean tire-spring force, which Phase 4.1 maintains (per D-06b).

</specifics>
