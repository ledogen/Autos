       - **Sprung Mass**: the portion of vehicle mass supported by the suspension springs (the body). In RangerSim: `mass · weightFront` for the front axle and `mass · weightRear` for the rear, halved per corner.
       - **Unsprung Mass**: the portion of vehicle mass NOT supported by the suspension springs (wheels, hubs, brakes). In RangerSim: `wheelMass` per corner (≈18 kg), integrated as `vehicleState.hubY[i]` / `hubVy[i]`.
       - **Suspension Travel**: the displacement of the wheel hub relative to the body mount point. Compression > 0 means the spring is shorter than rest; rest length is `suspensionRestLength{Front,Rear}`.
       - **Ride Height**: the height of the body above the ground at static equilibrium. In RangerSim: computed at spawn from `wheelRadius − tireCompressionRest + suspensionRestLength − suspensionCompressionRest` per RESEARCH §Pattern 4.
       - **Anti-Roll Bar (ARB)**: a torsional spring linking the left and right wheels of an axle that resists differential compression. In RangerSim: bilinear-spring approximation (D-07) with stiffness `arbStiffness{Front,Rear}`; force `F_arb = k · (suspComp[L] − suspComp[R])` applied as `−F_arb` to left, `+F_arb` to right.
       - **Substep / Physics Timestep Convention**: the outer physics step (`PHYSICS_DT = 1/60` s) integrates body 6DOF; the suspension vertical subsystem sub-steps at `PHYSICS_DT / 2` per D-08/D-09 for tire-spring stability. All future `1/60` literals must be replaced with `PHYSICS_DT`.
